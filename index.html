Articles Removed
Gambling & Betting — Arts 1012–1021

The entire section on gambling and betting (10 articles) covering invalidity, restitution, recoverability of losses, competitions, lotteries, and sports betting has been removed. Gambling regulation is now deferred to the General Commercial Gaming Regulatory Authority (GCGRA) specialized framework. Additional evidence provisions were moved to the separate Evidence Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 35 of 2022).

New Articles & Provisions
NEW
Art 121–122: Pre-Contractual Duties
Mandatory good faith in negotiations. Deliberate withholding of material information = bad faith. Void clauses limiting disclosure. Bad faith parties liable for actual damages (excluding lost profits unless agreed).
NEW
Art 138: Framework Agreements
Parties may predefine essential terms governing subsequent contracts. Unless otherwise agreed, framework agreements form part of definitive contracts. Critical for recurring/long-term commercial relationships.
NEW
Exceptional Circumstances Doctrine
Courts empowered to restore contractual equilibrium when unforeseeable circumstances cause imbalance. Options: extend performance, adjust remuneration, or order termination. Response to COVID-19 and inflation.
NEW
Single-Person Companies
Expressly permits establishment and ownership of a civil company by a single individual, confirming what was already allowed for commercial entities.
NEW
Professional Companies
Comprehensive framework for licensed professionals. Allows foreign firm partnerships and non-practitioner partners. Prohibits individuals from partnering in multiple professional companies.
NEW
Action to Stop New Works
New claim enabling property possessors to suspend construction activities that threaten their property rights.
NEW
Non-Profit Company Framework
New provisions introduced for non-profit civil companies, previously unaddressed in the civil code.
NEW
Company Dissolution on Withdrawal
Automatic dissolution upon partner withdrawal. Remaining partners may continue by mutual agreement. Manager authority terminates immediately upon dissolution; personal liability for post-dissolution acts.

Important Notes

Effective date: The new law takes effect on 1 June 2026. Until then, the 1985 law remains in force.

Approximate article numbers: The exact article numbers in the 2025 law for Books Two through Four are based on the Lexis Middle East comparison table and multiple law firm analyses. The full official English text should be consulted for precise references.

Evidence provisions: Many evidence-related articles from the 1985 law (Arts 112–123) have been moved to the separate Federal Decree-Law No. 35 of 2022 (Law of Evidence in Civil and Commercial Transactions).

Specialist law deference: The 2025 law deliberately excludes matters now governed by specialized laws (Companies Law, Bankruptcy Law, Gaming regulations) to reduce duplication.

This comparison is based on the PDF text of Federal Law No. 5 of 1985 and publicly available analyses of Federal Decree-Law No. 25 of 2025. Article mappings for Books II–IV are approximate. Consult the official gazette for authoritative text.
